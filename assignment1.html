Critique on a chart

I use the report from UNCTAD: https://unctad.org/publication/digital-economy-report-2024

Chart Title:

“Internet of Things devices forecast to surge amid digital economy boom”

The chart visualizes the forecast growth of IoT devices with cellular connections all over the world from 2016 to 2029. The data is presented as a stacked bar chart, with each color representing a different region.

Design and Layout:

  1. Chart Type:
    A stacked bar chart is a good choice for showing cumulative growth across regions.

  2. Readability:

    • Labels: The labels for the regions are clear, but the proximity of colors like “Northeast Asia” and “North America” may be confusing for readers.

    • Axes: The y-axis is labeled in increments of 2,000 million, but there’s no specific title on the y-axis itself. Adding a title (e.g., “Number of IoT Connections (in millions)”) would help to improve the readability.

  3. Color Usage:
    Generally OK.

  4. Layout:
    The chart’s layout is straightforward, with a clear temporal progression on the x-axis (years). However, the bars themselves are tightly packed, which limits white space and may cause the chart to feel cramped, especially as the values increase over time.

Data Representation and Accuracy:

  1. Scale and Proportions:
    The y-axis seems appropriately scaled, and the increments are even, which makes the chart easy to interpret at a glance. However, the full extent of changes in smaller regions, such as “Middle East and Africa” or “Latin America,” is lost due to the overwhelming size of “Northeast Asia.”

  2. Data Density:
    The chart tries to present a lot of data at once, and while it successfully shows the overall upward trend, some regional trends might not be as easily discernible due to the cumulative stacking. For instance, it is difficult to compare the growth rate of North America directly against Latin America, since the bars are layered on top of each other.

  3. Annotations:
    There are no annotations within the chart to highlight key points. Adding notes or callouts to significant milestones (e.g., largest growth in 2028, or key regional surges) would enhance the chart’s narrative. This would also provide context about why some regions might experience more growth than others.

Effectiveness in Conveying the Message:

  1. Narrative:
    The chart succeeds in conveying the general message that IoT device connections are forecasted to increase dramatically, with Northeast Asia being a key player. However, the story behind the growth in other regions is less clear, particularly due to the lack of annotations or context. Including a brief explanation within the chart could help clarify this.

  2. Interpretation:
    The chart is fairly intuitive, but only at a high level. The reader can easily see that Northeast Asia is leading the surge in IoT devices, but they may struggle to draw more nuanced insights about the relative growth of other regions.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  1. Color Palette:
    Adjust the colors for more contrast between regions, particularly for regions that currently have very similar shades (like Northeast Asia and North America). Using more distinct, vibrant colors would make it easier to distinguish between the regions.

  2. Chart Type:
    To give more visibility to smaller regions, consider using a grouped bar chart or a separate line chart for each region. This would allow for better comparison across regions without the distraction of the large-scale differences in Northeast Asia.

  3. Annotations:
    Adding annotations to highlight key trends (e.g., which regions are growing the fastest) would enhance the chart’s storytelling. Additionally, including context like the driving factors behind Northeast Asia’s dominance would provide more insight.

  4. Axes and Labels:
    Adding a y-axis title (e.g., “IoT Device Connections (in millions)”) would help provide context for the scale. The x-axis could also benefit from having more tick marks or labeling every year, not just alternating years.

Conclusion:

While this chart clearly shows the overall surge in IoT devices, it can be improved by tweaking the color palette, adding annotations, and considering alternative chart formats for better comparison across regions.
